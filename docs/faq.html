Q. Where do these forecasts come from?

A. The US National Weather Service's Short-Range Ensemble Forecast model.

Q. What does the number represent?

A. An estimate of much snow you're going to get over the next few days.

Q. Starting when? What if it's already snowing?

A. Starting now, the moment you refresh the page. The number only includes future snowfall, not snow already on the ground.

Q. Why don't you include the snow that's already on the ground?

A. We don't know how much snow is on the ground.

Q. Why not?

A. It's complicated. In some ways, the future is easier to consistently measure than the past.
